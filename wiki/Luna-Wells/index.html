Summary

Luna Wells is the titular point-of-view main character from Luna on the Run. She and her best friend Freckles, a steambot cat, end up in trouble after stealing the airship of notorious crime boss Slaphand, forcing Luna on an epic quest to rescue Freckles. Along the way, she displays her twisted morals and internal struggles.

Luna's story continues in later entries, such as The Blood Moon Pirate, where her willingness to do whatever it takes is explored deeper.

Early Life

Luna's early life was trademarked by three major events: her father taking her brother and leaving her and her mom in Carmsborough, her mom getting cancer, and being kicked out of her apartment after her mother's death. This led her to being a homeless child and living on the streets, while still working at the scrapyard.

On the one-year anniversary of her mother's death, she met Freckles at the scrapyard. Initially keeping Freckles to sell later, after he saves her from being ambushed, they become best friends.



Luna on the Run

In the beginning of Luna on the Run, Luna finds a beeping remote that leads her on a journey to find Shady Shane, a homeless person she sometimes makes trades with. The remote is revealed to belong to Slaphand, a crime boss in Carmsborough.

Upon confronting the crime boss to get money for the remote, she is chased, hijacks a cloudship, and nearly escapes, but activates a laser that destroys a dock. From there, she is captured, and her and Freckles are separated.

The duo are taken to Blood Moon Island, an island where Slaphand sends people who can't repay their debts, where Luna meets Mary, who inevitably helps her try to find Freckles and escape the island.

Once that plan backfires, they are trapped again by Slaphand, but the Steam War soon starts, and Syndra's men end up picking Luna up and taking her back to mainland Carmsborough.

From there, she works for Syndra and attempts to bring Slaphand down, has a close encounter with Orion and the Clockwork, and meets Agent Ike on her assassination mission.

Her and Agent Ike initially struggle to work together, but in the end, the two of them take down Slaphand's ship, and she kills Slaphand. Then, she rescues Freckles, and ends by getting revenge on the scrapyard.



The Blood Moon Pirate

In The Blood Moon Pirate, Luna works together with Freckles to take down some of Bijabers' underlings, and eventually aims for Bijabers himself.

Operating under the alias "The Blood Moon Pirate," Luna systematically assassinates multiple high-ranking Bijabers personnel in Carmsborough, drawing the attention of S6 and Orion and the Clockwork.

She faces Orion and the Clockwork multiple times, and ends up losing Bijabers due to their interference.



The Gearmaster's Apprentice

Luna is set to make an appearance in The Gearmaster's Apprentice, the third full-length book of the Carmsborough Vigilantes series.



Morality

The question of morality comes up multiple times during Luna's adventures. She tends to believe that there is nothing purely good, and that everything she is doing is for the greater good. As long as she is better than the person she is after, she is helping the world. This belief is challenged multiple times throughout the series, and is a point of debate between her and Freckles (in her mind) as well as her and Orion.
